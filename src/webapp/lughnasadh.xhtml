Lughnasadh

Lughnasadh is a traditional harvest festival. Our ancestors celebrated their good fortune in the harvest at Lughnasadh and it is a common tradition for hand-fastings, and other oaths and contracts to be taken at this time. In Irish mythology, Lugh, who symbolises the harvest, is brought to find a wife. None can be found and one is fashioned for him out of flowers. His flower bride is lured away by another and Lugh is killed in the ensuing battle. The killing of Lugh symbolises both the harvest, and the end of summer.

Games, Skills, Feast and Ritual

Lughnasadh is a celebration of the peak of the growing season, a chilled out community event, and a moment for reflection as summer begins to fade. Games of skill, some skill sharing workshops, a feast and a ritual are the central components of the event.

Drive very slowly on rough surfaces. Close all gates behind you.
South of Biggar, turn right onto the A72.
The turning right onto the track, is opposite a sign that says "Camp Road", just as you approach Symington.
Go past all the houses and keep going, there are some obvious parking spots 100 to 200 yards further down the track. Please do not block the track.. You can walk accross a field to the gate to the meadow. There should be a sign here to direct you.
On from the parking spots there is a sharp turn down a valley. This section of track is rougher, but most vehicles are fine to drive right down when its dry.
